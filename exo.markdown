<h3>Prior Work</h3>
<img src="/images/exo/exo.png" alt="Two-digit exoskeleton glove mechanism" loading="lazy" class="float-img-left">
<p>The aim of this research is to create a novel Exoskeleton Glove design, hereafter referred to as exo-glove, that is compact, lightweight, and repeatable. The proposed system could be used for assistance in performing the Activities of Daily Living (ADLs) as well as a rehabilitation tool for patients who have suffered from a stroke or brachial plexus injury.</p>
<p>Preliminary work led to the development of a single DOF linkage mechanism that is thin enough to be placed in between fingers, while being capable of enforcing natural bending angles on each of the phalanges. Being a single DOF mechanism, it only allows for achieving cylindrical grasp. The thumb had an additional degree of freedom, and this allowed for grasping objects within a wide range of sizes. A US patent has been granted on the developed exo-glove mechanism [3]. Detailed description and additional details on the mechanism can be found in [1].</p>
<div class="clearfix"></div>
<img src="/images/exo/sea.png" alt="Series elastic actuator design for exo-glove" loading="lazy" class="float-img-left">
<p>A five-finger version of the exo-glove was later built with series elastic actuation allowing for compliance and precise force control. The use of series elastic actuators (SEA) allows for precise force control on each finger. This allows the exo-glove to grasp objects of different stiffness, shape, and size. Additional details regarding the full five finger exo-glove and the SEA design can be found in [2].</p>

<div class="research-detail">
<h3>Ongoing Research</h3>
{% include youtube-video.html id="z1tforALG-8" title="Single finger exoskeleton mechanism with voice control" %}
<p>While the above system was an effective initial approach towards creating an assistive glove, the underlying mechanism for each finger has only a single DOF. As such the exo-glove could only be used to assist in achieving cylindrical grasp. Moreover, triggering the cylindrical grasp was achieved by the press of a button. Ongoing work on the exo-glove aims to improve the grasp capabilities of the previous design by incorporating two degrees of freedom in each finger. In addition, we are working on designing predictive controllers capable of recognizing user intent. This will result in a more naturalistic interaction between the user and the glove.</p>
